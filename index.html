Idle No More Final Project

History

Idle No More is a largely supported activist group protesting Canadian law bill #C-45, the jobs and growth act. Founded by: Jessica Gordon, Slyvia McAdam, Sheelah McLean, and Nina Wilson; this group of individuals started by directly exchanging emails which eventually led to the startup of their Facebook group “Idle No More”.

Goals

Some goals the movement has outlined in 2012 consisted of: repealing the Jobs and Growth act, encouraging more knowledge of indigenous sovereignty, awareness of indigenous peoples, and reducing non-environmentally friendly activities.

Officially Protested Government Bills & Acts

Idle No More dosen't protest the entire bill, just some laws inside the bill
  • Bill C-38 (Budget Bill #1)
    • Allows indigenous fisheries to be drained and used for mining
    • Environmental assessment is no longer required with any project fueled by federal funds
    • Removes environmental protection act from federal grounds, only the species that live within are protected
    • Government no longer has to publicize climate change policies
  • Bill C-45 (Budget Bill #2)
    • Cuts budget to indigenous reserve income
    • Allows constructions that would obstruct the “flow” of fish in the river
    • Non-licensed grain counter are now allowed to handle indigenous crops (Reserves not might get as much cash because of missed grain)
    • Removed age requirement for director of indegienous housing board (A child can now run your homes)
  • Bill C-27 (First Nations Financial Transparency Act)
    • Requires every dollar spent to now be documented in a annual report (Failure to adhere will result in fines)
    • An auditor must be present to document reports every month
    • Reserve is fined if the auditor does not get a report in on time
    • Individuals expenses and incomes ranges are documented in a public government database for 10 years
    • Failure to comply with the entire bill could result in lose all governmental funding
  • Bill S-2 (Family Homes on Reserve and Matrimonial Interests or Rights Act)
    • If the sovereign indigenous government doesn't quickly resolve an issue the supreme court takes over and takes care of it under their rules.
    • Household asset equalization must be approved by a community of a least 25% of eligible voters
    • Canadian charter laws are forced into First Nations sovereign laws
    • All sovereign laws must correspond with the Indian Act rules
  • Bill S-6 (First Nations Electrons Act)
    • Affects how chiefs and leaders of First Nations governments are elected
    • Created a prerequisite list for running as a candidate
  • Bill S-8 (Safe Drinking Water for First Nations)
    • No funding is actually provided to fulfill this act
    • Fails to respect First Nations way of life
    • Allows the government to replace your paid job with an unpaid water monitoring job
    • Reserves aren't allowed to borrow from provincial reserves without an extensive application process which can take 2 years for approval
  • Bill S-212 (First Nations Self-Government Recognition Bill)
    • States indigenous group languages will never be offically recognized as a language by the government
  • “First Nations” Private Ownership Act
    • Would allow true ownership of their land leases but the land couldn’t be used for anything, except under a contract with the government.
    • Would allow inheritance of land after owner passing, but under approval of government and new owner would sign on the existing contract
    • If no new lease holder is found government reclaims the land permanently

Impact on the public/government

The Idle No More movement has increased the public’s awareness of indigenous communities issues both in Canada and internationally. Nearly every indigenous community that has heard of the movement joins the movement hoping with a larger group their voices will be heard better. The movement also organizes and hosts many peaceful rallies and round dances. One of their largest events took place on January 28th, 2013. It consisted of 50 separate events that took place internationally. The media publicized the event and gained the movement even more recognition and support, leading to a meeting with Stephan Harper.

Meeting With Stephan Harper

Idle No More managed to have a meeting with former prime minister Stephan Harper after protesting in front of parliament for a month. To attract the attention of the media the protest’s elder went on a 28 day long liquid only diet, eating absolutely nothing. When the media heard of a protest involving indigenous rights they publicized the event and forced the government to issue a meeting from the resulting outrage of the public. The Idle No More group requested that the government allocate some of their resources to addressing indigenous issues such as: poverty, land claims, and profits from natural resources. Each of these requests were met with denial. Idle No More has since tried to get more meetings with the current prime minister with addressing these issues, and they did. But over half of the agreed upon terms have not been met.
